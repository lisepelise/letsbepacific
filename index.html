Our Purpose

We aim to educate individuals of all cultural backgrounds on ways to break generational curses, starting with a conversation.

Though simple sounding, these conversations are the very conversations our people try to avoid - the elephant in the room. This is a platform of safety, with no judgement. These difficult conversations aim to help improve relationships, and nuild stronger foundations for the future. Communication is key - as is every other virtual we have growing up; family, culture, tradition, respect etc.

Why is mental health important?

It is defined as our state of well-being, how we think, feel and behave. A 2017 studie found that Pacific Islander youths were more at risk of suicide than any other cultural group.

Our mental health is part of who we are. Having people by you is essential - you are not alone, even if it may feel like it.
